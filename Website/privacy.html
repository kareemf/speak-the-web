Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2026

URL Reader ("we", "our", or "the app") is designed with your privacy in mind. This policy explains how we handle your information.

Information We Collect

Short answer: Nothing.

URL Reader processes everything locally on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or store your personal data on any external servers.

Data Processed Locally

The following data is processed and stored only on your device:

  • URLs you enter: Stored locally in your recent articles list
  • Article content: Fetched from the web and cached locally for offline access
  • Reading progress: Saved locally so you can resume where you left off
  • Voice preferences: Your selected voice and speed settings
  • Downloaded voice models: Stored locally for offline text-to-speech

Network Requests

The app makes network requests in the following situations:

  • Fetching articles: When you enter a URL, we request that webpage to extract the article content
  • Downloading voice models: When you choose to download a neural voice, the model is fetched from GitHub

These requests go directly to the source (the article's website or GitHub for voice models). We do not proxy these requests through our own servers.

Third-Party Services

URL Reader does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. We do not share any data with third parties.

Voice Processing

All text-to-speech processing happens on your device:

  • System voices (AVSpeech): Use Apple's on-device speech synthesis
  • Neural voices (Sherpa/Piper): Use locally-downloaded AI models that run entirely on your device

Your article content is never sent to any speech service or cloud API.

iCloud and Backups

Your app data may be included in iOS device backups (iCloud or local). This is handled by iOS and is subject to Apple's privacy practices.

Data Retention

All data is stored locally on your device. You can delete this data at any time by:

  • Clearing your recent articles in the app
  • Deleting downloaded voice models in Settings
  • Deleting the app entirely

Children's Privacy

URL Reader does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. The app simply reads web content aloud and does not require any account or personal information to function.

Changes to This Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please contact us at support@example.com.
